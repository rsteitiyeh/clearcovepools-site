**The pool chemical for raising alkalinity is baking soda (sodium bicarbonate): about 1.5 lbs raises total alkalinity 10 ppm in 10,000 gallons. To lower alkalinity, use muriatic acid, added slowly to the deep end with the pump running. Target 80 to 120 ppm TA.**

What total alkalinity actually does

Total alkalinity is your water's shock absorber for pH. With TA in the 80 to 120 ppm range, pH drifts slowly and predictably. With TA at 40, every rainstorm and swimmer sends pH lurching around, and you burn through acid and soda chasing it.

Stable pH matters for more than comfort. The CDC's healthy swimming guidance keeps residential pools at pH 7.0 to 7.8 and free chlorine at 1 to 4 ppm (https://www.cdc.gov/healthy-swimming/safety/what-you-can-do-to-stay-healthy-in-swimming-pools.html), and chlorine kills germs fastest in that pH window. Alkalinity is what holds pH there between test days.

Raising alkalinity: baking soda is the answer

Sodium bicarbonate raises TA with only a mild upward nudge to pH, which is exactly what you want. The stuff sold as "alkalinity increaser" at pool stores is the same compound at two to three times the grocery price. Check the label: sodium bicarbonate or sodium hydrogen carbonate. Same molecule.

The core dosing rule: 1.5 lbs of baking soda raises TA by about 10 ppm in 10,000 gallons of water. Everything scales linearly from there.

Worked example 1: the typical backyard pool

Say you have a 15,000 gallon pool testing at TA 60 and you want 100. That is a 40 ppm raise. The math: (15,000 / 10,000) x 1.5 lbs x (40 / 10) = 9 lbs of baking soda. We would add 6 lbs, circulate for six hours, retest, then add the rest. Water rarely reads exactly like the formula, and it is easier to add more than to take it back out.

Worked example 2: the small above-ground pool

A 5,000 gallon above-ground pool at TA 85, target 100. That is a 15 ppm raise: (5,000 / 10,000) x 1.5 x (15 / 10) = 1.1 lbs, call it 18 ounces. A standard 1 lb grocery box gets you most of the way. This is also a raise small enough that we would only bother if pH had been drifting.

How to add it

Broadcast the powder across the deep end with the pump running, or pre-dissolve in a bucket of pool water for faster mixing. Limit single additions to about 10 lbs per 10,000 gallons, wait six hours, retest. Baking soda is about as benign as pool chemicals get, but wear gloves and avoid breathing the dust cloud.

Lowering alkalinity: muriatic acid, carefully

High TA (above roughly 140 ppm) makes pH stubborn and pushes water toward scaling. The fix is muriatic acid, which drops both pH and TA. There is no chemical that lowers TA without touching pH, so the process is a cycle: acid drops both, then aeration brings pH back up without raising TA.

Rule of thumb: about 26 fluid ounces of full-strength muriatic acid (31.45 percent) lowers TA by roughly 10 ppm in 10,000 gallons. It will also pull pH down noticeably, which is why we work in stages.

Worked example 3: bringing 180 down to 110

A 10,000 gallon pool at TA 180, target 110, needs a 70 ppm drop. Total acid: 26 oz x 7 = about 182 oz, or 1.4 gallons. Never add that at once. We do 32 oz, run the pump, retest pH after four hours, and only add the next round once pH has recovered above 7.2. Point returns up and run a fountain or waterfall if you have one; aeration speeds the pH rebound.

Expect this to take one to two weeks of cycles. Anyone promising a one-day TA crash is also promising you a pH of 6.8 and itchy swimmers.

Acid safety is not optional

Always add acid to water, never water to acid. Adding water to concentrated acid can spit and splatter violently. Wear chemical-resistant gloves and goggles, work upwind, and keep acid stored away from chlorine products; the two must never mix. Pour slowly into the deep end with the pump running, and rinse your measuring cup in the pool, not the deck.

Dosing cheat sheet

|                              |                                  |                            |                              |
| ---------------------------- | -------------------------------- | -------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| **Goal**                     | **Chemical**                     | **Dose per 10,000 gal**    | **Effect**                   |
| Raise TA 10 ppm              | Baking soda (sodium bicarbonate) | 1.5 lbs                    | TA up, pH nudges up slightly |
| Raise TA 10 ppm, pH also low | Soda ash (sodium carbonate)      | 12 oz (raises pH strongly) | TA and pH both up            |
| Lower TA 10 ppm              | Muriatic acid, 31.45%            | About 26 fl oz, in stages  | TA and pH both down          |
| Recover pH after acid        | Aeration (fountain, returns up)  | Run until pH 7.4 to 7.6    | pH up, TA unchanged          |

One clarification on soda ash: it is the right pick only when pH and TA are both low. Use it to fix TA alone and you will overshoot pH, then need acid, then be back where you started with lighter pockets.

Why your TA keeps drifting

Trichlor tablets are acidic and grind TA down week after week; if you feed tablets, plan on periodic baking soda. Our breakdown at ClearCove Pools/pool-chlorine-tablets covers how much acidity each tablet adds. Liquid chlorine and cal-hypo push the other direction, slowly raising pH.

How TA, pH, and CYA fit together

A quick note on test order, because it changes what you dose. If you use trichlor tablets, part of your measured TA is actually cyanurate alkalinity from the stabilizer. At CYA 50 and pH 7.5, roughly 15 ppm of your TA reading is cyanurate, not bicarbonate buffer. A pool testing TA 90 with CYA 100 has less real buffering than the number suggests.

For most backyard owners the practical takeaway is simple: keep CYA in the 30 to 50 ppm range recommended for chlorine pools, and the standard 80 to 120 ppm TA target works as printed. If CYA has crept toward 100, aim for the top of the TA range and plan a partial drain to bring stabilizer down.

Salt pools run their own wrinkle. Salt cells generate chlorine in a way that pushes pH up steadily, so many salt pool owners hold TA nearer 70 to 90 to slow the climb and dose small amounts of acid weekly. Test both, weekly, and let the trend tell you your pool's habit.

FAQ

How long does baking soda take to raise alkalinity?

The chemistry is nearly instant once the powder dissolves; full distribution takes about six hours of pump runtime in a typical residential pool. Retest after that window, not before. Cloudiness after a large dose is normal and clears within a day as the bicarbonate finishes dissolving and circulating.

Is baking soda the same as pool alkalinity increaser?

Yes. Alkalinity increaser is sodium bicarbonate, chemically identical to grocery store baking soda. The pool store version costs $2 to $4 per pound; grocery baking soda in bulk runs well under $1 per pound. Buy whichever is cheaper per pound near you, and dose by the same 1.5 lbs per 10 ppm per 10,000 gallon rule.

Can I swim right after adding baking soda?

Wait until the powder has fully dissolved and circulated, usually 30 to 60 minutes with the pump running. After muriatic acid, wait longer: at least 4 hours and a retest showing pH between 7.2 and 7.8 before anyone gets in. When in doubt, test first, swim second.

What happens if alkalinity is too low?

Below about 60 ppm, pH loses its buffer and swings sharply with rain, swimmers, and chlorine additions. Sustained low TA and low pH corrode heat exchangers, etch plaster, and fade vinyl liners. Chlorine also becomes harsh and inefficient outside the 7.2 to 7.8 pH window, so sanitation suffers along with the equipment.

Will lowering alkalinity also lower my pH?

Yes, unavoidably. Muriatic acid drops both at once. The trick is the cycle: dose acid, let TA fall with pH, then aerate the water so pH climbs back while TA stays put. Repeat until TA lands in range. It takes days, not hours, and that is normal.

Bottom line

Baking soda up at 1.5 lbs per 10 ppm per 10,000 gallons, muriatic acid down in staged doses, and retest before every addition. Hold TA between 80 and 120 ppm and most of your pH problems quietly disappear.
